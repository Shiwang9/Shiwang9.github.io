My Fallen Angel


She had a dream,
Though it did not pan out as scheme.
It shattered, leaving her battered.
She picked herself up,
and entrusted it to her future,
In lieu that her wound will get a suture.

Countless days she toiled hard,
Just so that the little one,
Learned to stay on guard.
At nights she remained awake,
Teaching the ignorant to handle the atrocities;
That life leaves in its wake.
It was her who kept breaking it, tasking it,
Steeling it, recasting it.

The no longer little one developed a perception;
Which stated that its life needed an interjection.
Things were not normal,
They had to be made formal.
Rebellion broke both their backs;
Wall of the connection developed cracks.

War always comes at a price.
The desire for independence,
Did nothing to the cord of the bond,
But slice.
Yet her belief did not waiver,
The fruits of her labor,
The grown one would savour.

But to the grown one,
The realisation dawned upon a little late.
To her soul the devilish one,
Had done nothing but mutilate.


So now the grown one says,
She made broken look beautiful
She made strong look invincible
She walked with the universe on her shoulders
and made it appear like a pair of wings.
